**First-Order Predicate Logic – Syntax and Semantics** First-order logic (FOL), also known as predicate logic or first-order predicate logic, is an extension of propositional logic used in artificial intelligence for knowledge representation. Unlike propositional logic, which can only represent facts as true or false, FOL can represent complex sentences and natural language statements. It's more expressive, allowing representation of statements like "Some humans are intelligent" or "Sachin likes cricket," which are impossible to represent using only propositional logic. FOL assumes the world contains: * **Objects:** A, B, people, numbers, colors, wars, theories, squares, pits, wumpus, etc. * **Relations:** These can be unary (e.g., red, round, is adjacent) or n-ary (e.g., sister of, brother of, has color, comes between). * **Functions:** Father of, best friend, third inning of, end of, etc. Like natural language, FOL has two main parts: * **Syntax:** The rules for forming well-formed formulas (WFFs).
